Effective Daily Routine

A structured daily routine is essential for consistent and effective preparation. This template provides a balanced approach to cover all aspects of the Nepal Police Technical ASI exam while maintaining physical and mental well-being.

How to Use This Schedule

This daily schedule template can be adjusted based on your personal circumstances, strengths, and weaknesses. The key is to maintain consistency and balance across all preparation areas.

You can modify the time allocations while ensuring you cover all necessary components each day.

Phase-Specific Adjustments

As you progress through the 12-week plan, you should adjust your daily schedule to align with the changing focus areas:

  • Theoretical Study: Allocate more time (3 hours) to build a strong foundation
  • MCQ Practice: Focus on understanding concepts rather than speed
  • Typing Practice: Emphasize correct technique over speed
  • Practical Skills: Basic familiarization with all applications
  • Physical Fitness: Start with moderate intensity and gradually increase
  • Interview Preparation: Research about Nepal Police and basic interview questions

  • Theoretical Study: Balance between new topics and revision (2.5 hours)
  • MCQ Practice: Implement negative marking strategy and increase question volume
  • Typing Practice: Focus on improving speed while maintaining accuracy
  • Practical Skills: More complex exercises and timed practice
  • Physical Fitness: Maintain consistent routine with moderate intensity
  • Interview Preparation: Practice responses to common questions

  • Theoretical Study: Focus on revision and weak areas (2 hours)
  • MCQ Practice: Full-length timed tests with strict negative marking
  • Typing Practice: Timed tests simulating exam conditions
  • Practical Skills: Complete practical exam simulations
  • Physical Fitness: Maintain routine but avoid excessive exertion
  • Interview Preparation: Mock interviews and advanced preparation

Productivity Tips

  • Use the Pomodoro Technique (25 minutes study, 5 minutes break)
  • Stay hydrated throughout the day
  • Take short eye breaks every 20-30 minutes
  • Use active recall techniques instead of passive reading
  • Create a distraction-free study environment
  • Use spaced repetition for better retention
  • Maintain a consistent sleep schedule
